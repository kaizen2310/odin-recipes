KOTHIMBIR WADI

Description

Kothimbir Vadi is a crispy and flavorful Maharashtrian snack made from besan (gram flour) and fresh coriander leaves. The batter, infused with spices like turmeric, cumin, and sesame seeds, is first steamed to form a solid block and then sliced into pieces, which are shallow or deep-fried until crispy. The vadis have a deliciously nutty and earthy flavor with a slight crunch. This dish is a perfect tea-time snack and can be served with green chutney or ketchup. It is often enjoyed as an appetizer or side dish in Maharashtrian households.

Ingridients

  • cup besan (gram flour)
  • cup coriander leaves, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • teaspoon sesame seeds
  • teaspoon turmeric powder
  • teaspoon red chili powder
  • teaspoon cumin powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Batter
    • a bowl, mix besan, coriander leaves, green chilies, sesame seeds, turmeric, red chili powder, cumin powder, baking soda, salt, and ginger-garlic paste.
    • Add water gradually to form a thick batter.
  2. Steam the Vadi
    • Grease a plate and pour the batter into it.
    • Steam for 15–20 minutes until firm.
    • Let it cool and cut into pieces.
  3. Fry the Vadi
    • Heat oil in a pan and shallow or deep-fry the vadis until golden brown.
    • Serve hot with green chutney or ketchup.
