Misal PAv

descritption

Misal Pav is a traditional Maharashtrian dish known for its spicy and tangy flavors. It is made with sprouted legumes cooked in a spicy, aromatic gravy known as "kat" or "tarri." The dish is topped with farsan (crunchy fried mixture), chopped onions, tomatoes, and fresh coriander. It is served with soft pav (bread rolls), which helps balance the spice level. There are various regional variations of misal, such as Puneri Misal, Kolhapuri Misal, and Nashik Misal, each with its unique level of spiciness and method of preparation. This dish is commonly eaten as a hearty breakfast or snack

Ingridients

For Usal(sprout curry)

  • 2 cups mixed sprouts (matki, moong, chana)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1½ teaspoons red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon goda masala or garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water

For Kat(spicy gravy)

  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons kanda-lasun (onion-garlic) masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 cup water

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Usal
    • Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard and cumin seeds.
    • Add onions and sauté until golden brown.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell goes away.
    • Add tomatoes, turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and goda masala. Cook until tomatoes soften.
    • Add sprouts, mix well, and add water.
    • Cover and simmer for 10–15 minutes
  2. Prepare the Kat (Spicy Gravy)
    • Heat oil in a separate pan, add mustard and cumin seeds.
    • Add kanda-lasun masala, red chili powder, turmeric, and water. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Assemble the Misal Pav
    • In a bowl, add a serving of usal.
    • Pour the spicy kat over it.
    • Top with farsan, chopped onions, tomatoes, and coriander.
    • Serve hot with buttered pav and a lemon wedge.
