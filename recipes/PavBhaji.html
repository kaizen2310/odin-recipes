PAV BHAJI

Description

Pav Bhaji is one of the most popular street foods of Maharashtra. It consists of a spicy, flavorful mashed vegetable curry (bhaji) served with buttered and toasted pav (soft bread rolls). Originally a quick meal for textile workers in Mumbai, it has now become a beloved dish across India. The bhaji is made using potatoes, tomatoes, peas, capsicum, and a special blend of pav bhaji masala, giving it a rich, tangy, and slightly spicy taste. It is typically garnished with butter, fresh coriander, chopped onions, and a squeeze of lemon juice, adding extra layers of flavor.

Ingridients

For bhaji

  • 3 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 cup cauliflower, finely chopped
  • ½ cup green peas
  • 1 medium carrot, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 medium capsicum, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1½ tablespoons pav bhaji masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • ½ lemon (for garnishing)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped

For pav

  • 8 pav (bread rolls)
  • tablespoons butter
  • teaspoon pav bhaji masala

Instructions

  1. Boil the Vegetables
    • a pressure cooker, add potatoes, cauliflower, peas, and carrots.
    • Add enough water and cook for 3–4 whistles.
    • done, mash the vegetables and keep them aside.
  2. Prepare the Bhaji
    • butter and oil in a pan.
    • Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies; sauté until the raw smell disappears.
    • Add chopped capsicum and cook for 2 minutes.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
    • Add turmeric, chili powder, and pav bhaji masala. Mix well.
    • Add the mashed vegetables and mix everything together.
    • Add water as needed to adjust consistency. Simmer for 5–7 minutes.
    • Mash the bhaji well and garnish with coriander leaves
  3. Toast the Pav
    • Heat butter on a tawa (flat pan).
    • Sprinkle pav bhaji masala over the butter.
    • Slice the pav in half and toast until golden brown.
  4. Serve
    • Serve hot bhaji with buttered pav.
    • Garnish with a dollop of butter, chopped onions, and lemon wedges
