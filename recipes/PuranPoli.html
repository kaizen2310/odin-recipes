PURAN POLI

Description

Puran Poli is a traditional Maharashtrian sweet flatbread made during festivals like Gudi Padwa, Holi, and Diwali. The dish consists of a soft wheat flour dough stuffed with a sweet filling made of cooked chana dal (Bengal gram) and jaggery, flavored with cardamom and nutmeg. Once rolled out, the poli is cooked on a hot tawa with ghee, giving it a soft yet slightly crispy texture. It is often served with warm milk, a dollop of ghee, or even amti (spicy dal) in some regions. This delicacy has a rich, comforting sweetness and a melt-in-the-mouth texture.

Ingridients

For the Stuffing (Puran)

  • 1 cup chana dal (split Bengal gram)
  • 1 cup jaggery, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg powder (optional)

For the Dough (Poli)

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee
  • A pinch of salt
  • Water as needed

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Puran (Stuffing)
    • Rinse and cook chana dal in a pressure cooker until soft.
    • Drain excess water and mash the dal.
    • In a pan, add mashed dal and grated jaggery. Cook until it thickens.
    • Add cardamom and nutmeg powder. Let it cool.
  2. Prepare the Dough
    • Mix wheat flour, oil, salt, and water. Knead into a soft dough.
    • Cover and rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Stuff and Roll
    • Divide the dough into equal portions.
    • Flatten each ball and place a portion of puran inside.
    • Seal the edges and roll it into a flat roti.
  4. Cook the Puran Poli
    • Heat a tawa and cook the poli on medium heat.
    • Flip and cook both sides, applying ghee.
    • Serve warm with ghee or milk.
